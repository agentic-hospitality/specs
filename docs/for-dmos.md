# Curator for DMOs

**A one-page explainer for destination marketers, tourism boards, and hospitality leaders**

---

## The Problem

AI is changing how people discover and book travel.

When someone asks ChatGPT, Claude, or Google's AI "Where should I stay in Norfolk?", they don't get a list of ten options to compare. They get **one answer**. Maybe two.

AI doesn't browse. It doesn't compare. It **chooses**.

This creates a new challenge: How does an AI decide which venue to recommend? Right now, it relies on:
- Whatever it learned during training (often outdated)
- Whatever it can scrape from the web (often marketing fluff)
- Whatever big platforms tell it (often biased toward their inventory)

**What's missing:** The regional expertise, local knowledge, and trusted curation that organisations like yours have spent decades building.

---

## The Opportunity

You already know your region better than any algorithm. You know:
- Which hotel is actually good (not just well-SEO'd)
- Which restaurant the locals love (not just the tourist traps)
- Which hidden gem deserves more attention

The problem isn't knowledge. It's **translation**.

AI agents can't read your brochures. They can't interpret your website's prose. They need structured data that answers specific questions:
- Who are you? (A DMO? A hotel group? A guide?)
- What venues do you vouch for?
- Why should I trust your recommendations?

**Curator is that translation layer.**

---

## What Curator Does

Curator is an open specification that lets organisations like yours become **queryable authorities** for AI agents.

Instead of hoping AI stumbles across your website, you become a trusted source it actively consults.

**Three things Curator enables:**

### 1. Verification
You confirm that venues exist and belong to your region. AI agents use this as proof, not just claims.

### 2. Stories
This is where the real value lies. You interview the people, capturing the insider details, the human texture, the things only locals know. Why does the chef source from that farm? What happens at the bar on Friday nights? Who actually comes here? These become evidence AI agents can cite when making recommendations.

### 3. Answers
AI agents can query you directly: "Where should I stay near the Norfolk coast?" You answer. Your expertise flows directly into the recommendation.

---

## Why This Matters Now

The window is open but closing.

Right now, AI agents are learning who to trust. The organisations that establish themselves as authoritative sources early will be the ones AI consults by default.

Those who don't will become invisible, not because they lack expertise, but because they never translated it into a language AI understands.

---

## The Mutual Benefit

Curator creates a loop where everyone wins:

**For you:**
- Your expertise reaches AI-driven discovery
- Your members/venues get recommended by trusted association
- You become essential infrastructure, not just a marketing channel

**For venues:**
- Third-party validation they couldn't buy
- Stories that surface them to AI agents
- Access to travellers who never would have found them otherwise

**For travellers:**
- Better recommendations from people who actually know the region
- Hidden gems surfaced alongside obvious choices
- Trust in recommendations backed by real expertise

---

## What We're Not Asking

We're not asking you to:
- Replace your website or existing systems
- Change how you work with members
- Pay for another platform

Curator is an open specification. You implement it on your own infrastructure. You control your data. You remain the authority.

We're simply offering a **standard way to express what you already know** so AI agents can use it.

---

## The Core Insight

> "The destination doesn't describe itself. Its people do."

AI doesn't need more marketing copy. It needs authentic voices, local expertise, and third-party verification.

You have all three. Curator helps you share them.
